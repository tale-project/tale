title: Editor role

An **Editor** is the content-management role. You are the person who curates what the AI knows and decides which pending actions go through — the documents, the products, the customers, the websites that the rest of the organisation reads from, plus the customer conversations and approvals that need a human in the loop. Everything a Member can do, you can do, and on top of that you write to the knowledge base and act on approvals. You do not create agents, publish automations, or change organisation settings — those are Developer and Admin territory.

The point of having a dedicated Editor role is that knowledge curation is its own job. An agent built by a Developer is only as good as the documents it can ground in; an automation that creates draft replies is only as useful as the Editor who reviews and sends them. This page is a task-oriented index for the editor's day; the canonical permission matrix lives at [Members and roles](/platform/admin/members-and-roles).

## What Editors typically do

- Upload a document to the [knowledge base](/platform/workspace/knowledge-base) so agents can answer from it.
- Crawl a website and keep it refreshed — see [website crawling](/platform/knowledge/crawling).
- Create or edit a [product, customer, or vendor](/platform/knowledge/structured-data) to enrich the AI's grounded answers.
- Reply to a customer conversation under [Conversations](/platform/workspace/conversations).
- Approve or reject a pending action in [Approvals](/platform/workspace/approvals).

## What Editors cannot do

Creating or editing agents, publishing automations, configuring integrations, and changing organisation settings are gated to Developers and Admins. If you need one of those done, request it from someone with the right role — building an agent without an Editor on the team is harder than the reverse.

## Where to start

If you are stepping into the role today, the smallest useful first move is to open the [knowledge base](/platform/workspace/knowledge-base), upload one document the team already references daily, and confirm the AI can answer a question from it. From there, [build your first agent end to end](/tutorials/editor/first-agent-end-to-end) is the tutorial that closes the loop between curated knowledge and an AI surface that uses it.
